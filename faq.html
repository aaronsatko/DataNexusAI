In this hackathon, participants are provided with a sample dataset of 20 songs with known genres. Your task is to craft a prompt that, when used with an LLM such as ElonGPT, will accurately categorize songs into their respective genres. The final evaluation is performed on a blind test set of 100 songs.

Scoring is based on the number of correct genre classifications, and the format of your answer is critical. This challenge is designed to test your skills in prompt engineering and your ability to generalize from a small sample.

You can use any model or computational approach available, as long as your solution is fully automated and reproducible. Manual corrections or human-assisted outputs are not permitted.

Your submission must consist solely of your engineered prompt for song categorization. You will have access to a sample dataset of 20 songs (testable using ElonGPT), while the blind test set of 100 songs will be used for scoring.

No previous machine learning or programming experience is required! This hackathon is centered on creative prompt engineering and is designed to be accessible to participants from diverse backgrounds. The sample dataset is provided in an Excel format to facilitate ease of use.

The competition will begin on February 17th, 2025, when the sample dataset of 20 songs will be made available for testing with ElonGPT.

The last day for submissions will be March 16th, 2025. Final evaluations will be conducted on the blind test set of 100 songs.

You can sign up any time before the final submission deadline. Early registration is encouraged to give you ample time to develop your prompt.

The top 3 teams, based on the number of correct genre classifications, will win prizes. Winners may also be invited to showcase their prompt engineering approach during a special session at the Data Nexus conference.

You can share the competition data only with members of your team. By participating, you agree not to share the provided datasets with anyone outside your team until after the competition concludes.
